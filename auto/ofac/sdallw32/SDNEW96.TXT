As a result of the General Framework Agreement for Peace in
Bosnia and Herzegovina initialled in Dayton, Ohio on November 21,
1995, following up on Resolution No. 1022 of the United Nations
Security Council, and pursuant to Presidential Determination No.
96-7 of December 27, 1995, sanctions involving the Federal
Republic of Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro) have been
prospectively suspended.  Transactions involving the following
entries, listed with "[FRY S&M]" on the alphabetical master list
of Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons, need no
longer be interdicted and U.S. persons are free to deal with the
named individuals and entities.  Property and interests in
property which were blocked before December 27, 1995 must remain
blocked except as authorized, in general license or otherwise, by
the Office of Foreign Assets Control.

Sanctions are still in place with regard to Bosnian Serb forces
and authorities and the areas of the Republic of Bosnia and
Herzegovina that they control.  Transactions, therefore, with
entries listed with "[SRBH]" on the alphabetical master list of
Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons are still
prohibited (and appropriately interdicted) in addition to assets
remaining blocked. 

---------------

Blocked vessels for every sanctions program, designated by
"(vessel)" in individual entries, have been segregated into a
separate section on the Master list of Specially Designated
Nationals and Blocked Persons.  Banks are now instructed to
reject any funds transfer referencing a blocked vessel and must
notify OFAC, via facsimile with a copy of the payment
instructions, that funds have been returned to remitter due to
the possible involvement of an SDN vessel in the underlying
transaction.  Banks must contact OFAC's Compliance Programs
Division for further instructions should the name of an SDN
vessel appear in shipping documents presented under a letter of
credit or if noticed in a documentary collection.  SDN vessels
must themselves be physically blocked should they enter U.S.
jurisdiction.  Freight forwarders and shippers may not charter,
book cargo on, or otherwise deal with SDN vessels.
